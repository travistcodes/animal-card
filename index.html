Cardinal

Cardinals have as many as 16 different calls according to some scientists.

  • Scientific Name: Cardinalidae
  • Average Length: 21-23.5 cm
  • Average Lifespan: 3 years
  • Habitat: Cardinals (Cardinalis cardinalis) are abundant throughout nearly half of the United States. Their range spans a wide variety of habitats.

Cardinal birds are long-tailed songbirds with a crest on their heads and a short, thick, red-orange bill. They are named after the Roman Catholic Bishops because of their red color. Male cardinals are bright red, while female cardinals are pale brown with some red accents.
